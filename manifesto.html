The Surface

On October 31, 2008, a pseudonymous figure named Satoshi Nakamoto posted a nine-page paper to a mailing list of a few hundred cryptographers. The subject line read: "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System."

The world read it as digital money. A way to send value without a bank. A new kind of currency. And that's true — but it's the surface.

Everyone built on the surface. Exchanges. ETFs. Trading platforms. Price tickers. The entire industry looked at the ocean and saw only waves.

Nobody dove.
